2. What is Data Science?
Answer: Data Science is an interdisciplinary field that uses scientific methods, processes, algorithms, and systems to extract knowledge and insights from large amounts of raw, structured, and unstructured data. It combines statistics, data analysis, and machine learning to interpret data and generate meaningful insights.

1. How is AI related to Data Science?
Answer: Data Science provides the foundation for AI. Data scientists collect, clean, and analyze data to find patterns. AI, particularly machine learning, uses this processed data to train models that can make predictions or decisions. Data science is about understanding data, while AI is about building intelligent agents that use that understanding to act.

2. Explain the difference between supervised and unsupervised learning.
Answer: Supervised learning uses labeled data to train models for prediction tasks like classification and regression. Unsupervised learning works with unlabeled data to find hidden patterns, used for tasks like clustering and dimensionality reduction.


Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a branch of computer science that focuses on developing machines and software capable of mimicking human intelligence, enabling them to learn, reason, perceive, and solve problems. This field has evolved from a theoretical concept to a transformative force woven into the fabric of modern life, powering everything from virtual assistants and search engines to self-driving cars and medical diagnostics. At its core, AI operates by processing and learning from vast amounts of data to identify patterns and make informed decisions, often without explicit programming for every possibility. The current evolution of AI is largely driven by advances in sub-fields like machine learning, deep learning, and natural language processing, which allow computers to adapt and improve their performance over time. While AI offers significant benefits such as increased efficiency and innovation, its rapid development also raises important ethical considerations regarding data privacy, bias, and its impact on the future of employment.
